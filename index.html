The Childcare Constraint

Working paper — preparing for submission

Estimating the causal effect of childcare access on labor supply in Central Minnesota

RESEARCH QUESTIONDoes access to licensed childcare increase labor-force participation, employment, and full-time work among parents of young children?

  • Responses diverge sharply by household type: a significant negative labor-force participation response among single mothers in higher-supply ZIP codes, alongside a positive employment response among single fathers.
  • Full-time employment shows a large discontinuity at the CCAP subsidy-eligibility threshold — evidence that how assistance phases out matters as much as how much childcare exists. RD estimates are reported with robustness caveats.

DATA  20 years of MN DHS/DCYF licensing records × ACS PUMS microdata, N ≈ 103,000 · MN DEED · QCEW · FRED
METHODS  DiD/TWFE (HonestDiD) · IV · RD at the CCAP threshold   SOFTWARE  R (fixest, rdrobust, ivreg, did)

Buffer Depletion and Price Instability

Working paper

The inventory channel of monetary policy in U.S. manufacturing

RESEARCH QUESTIONDo interest-rate shocks affect inventory behavior and producer price volatility in inventory-intensive sectors?

  • After a contractionary monetary shock, inventory-intensive sectors show a significant, persistent rise in inventory-to-shipments ratios — peaking near +0.4 and staying elevated for roughly 18 months — as shipments contract faster than inventories can adjust.
  • Producer price volatility rises more in inventory-intensive sectors, peaking about six months post-shock. A 12-month placebo shock shows no comparable pattern, supporting a causal interpretation.

METHODS  Jordà local projections · two-way fixed effects · placebo & split-sample robustness   SOFTWARE  R, full reproducible pipeline

Oil Prices and Geopolitical Risk

Working paper + published article

What 35 years of data say about crude oil and geopolitical uncertainty

RESEARCH QUESTIONDoes geopolitical uncertainty move oil prices — or oil-price volatility?

  • A two-state Markov-switching model identifies a distinct high-volatility "crisis" regime, with probabilities spiking around the 1990 Gulf War, 2008, 2014–16, the 2020 pandemic shock, and 2022.
  • Local projections show a geopolitical risk shock is followed by a modest cumulative decline in WTI returns (~0.6% over the following month) rather than the price spike conventional wisdom predicts — geopolitical risk manifests as volatility and regime-switching, not persistent price increases.

DATA  Caldara–Iacoviello GPR Index · WTI · macro indicators   METHODS  Markov-switching · EGARCH · Jordà local projections   SOFTWARE  R

Spillover Effects of U.S. Fiscal Policy on OECD Monetary Policy

M.S. thesis — St. Cloud State University

How U.S. fiscal shocks transmit to central bank decisions in 30+ economies

RESEARCH QUESTIONHow do expansionary U.S. fiscal shocks influence short-term policy rates across OECD economies?

  • Responses diverge systematically: high-income OECD economies tend to respond to a U.S. fiscal stimulus shock with expansionary monetary policy, while other OECD economies are more likely to tighten facing the same shock.
  • Two-stage design: a Cholesky-identified SVAR extracts U.S. structural shocks (fiscal, IS, aggregate supply, monetary); country-level ARDL models then trace each economy's dynamic policy-rate response to a one-unit fiscal shock.

DATA  OECD Statistics · FRED · national accounts   METHODS  SVAR · ARDL · cointegration · IRFs · FEVD   SOFTWARE  R, Stata

Order Cadence Model

Industry project — Bay State Milling

A quantitative decision-support framework for procurement and inventory planning

OBJECTIVEWhen should we order, and how much? Forecasting and inventory theory combined into a tool procurement teams actually used.

  • Integrated demand forecasts, inventory position, pipeline inventory, safety stock, lead-time uncertainty, and purchasing schedules into a single planning framework.
  • Improved planning accuracy by ~25% through statistically driven forecasting and reduced supply-chain risk by aligning order timing with demand and lead-time variability.

METHODS  forecasting · inventory analytics · operations research   SOFTWARE  Excel, SQL, R
